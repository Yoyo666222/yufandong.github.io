<p class="page-deck">I study how coordinated activity across the cortex creates distinct brain states—and what REM sleep can reveal about cognition and consciousness.</p>

<div class="research-feature"><p class="profile-kicker">Ongoing · 2024–present</p><h2>Spontaneous orofacial dynamics during REM sleep</h2><p>Investigating how eye movements and other spontaneous orofacial events are represented across the cortex during REM sleep.</p></div>

## Research directions

<div class="research-grid research-grid--page">
  <article class="research-card"><span class="research-card__number">01</span><h3>Cortical dynamics of REM sleep</h3><p>Mapping cortex-wide activity and identifying mechanisms that regulate transitions between REM sleep substages.</p></article>
  <article class="research-card"><span class="research-card__number">02</span><h3>REM sleep & creative cognition</h3><p>Developing behavioral paradigms to test the role of REM sleep in creative problem solving and higher cognition.</p></article>
  <article class="research-card"><span class="research-card__number">03</span><h3>Consciousness across brain states</h3><p>Exploring how inter-layer cortical communication, temporal hierarchy, and spatial integration support conscious experience.</p></article>
  <article class="research-card"><span class="research-card__number">04</span><h3>Comparative cortical computation</h3><p>Asking how cortical computation in cognitive processes has evolved across species.</p></article>
</div>
